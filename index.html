Need for Green Energy Production

The energy supply sector is the largest contributor to global greenhouse gas (GHG) emissions where rapid economic growth (with the associated higher demand for power, heat, and transport services) and an increase of the share of coal in the global fuel mix are the main contributors to this trend. Therefore the swift adoption of renewable energy solutions constitute safe, reliable and affordable pathways capable of achieving the energy- related carbon-dioxide (CO2) emission reductions required to meet both nationally and globally pledged climate change goals.

Technologies

Our Renewable Energy consultancy services comprise a heterogeneous class of technologies which include the following

Solar Energy

Solar Energy technology harnesses the energy of solar irradiance to produce electricity using photovoltaics (PV) and concentrating solar power (CSP), to produce thermal energy (heating or cooling, either through passive or active means), to meet direct lightingneeds and, potentially, to produce fuels that might be used for transport and other purposes.

Learn More

Wind Energy

Wind Energy harnesses the kinetic energy of moving air. The primary application of relevance to climate change mitigation is to produce electricity from large wind turbines located on land (onshore) or in sea- or freshwater (offshore).

Learn More

Hydropower

Hydropower harnesses the energy of water moving from higher to lower elevations, primarily to generate electricity. Hydropower projects encompass dam projects with reservoirs, run-of-river and in-stream projects and cover a continuum in project scale.

Learn More
